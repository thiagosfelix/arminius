Jacobus Arminius

Theologian and Pastor

Chronology

  1. 1559

    Birth

    Jacobus Arminius was born in Oudewater, Netherlands.

  2. 1572

    First studies

    Jacobus Arminius started his education at Utrecht.

  3. 1575

    Move and tragedy

    Rudolph Snelius take Arminius to Marburg to study. In the same year, Arminius family is murdered in the spanish attack to Oudewater.

  4. 1576

    Admission in Leiden

    Arminius goes to Rotterdam and enrols in Leiden

  5. 1582

    Admission in Geneva

    Arminius enrols in Geneva, where he studies under Theodore Beza.

  6. 1583

    Basel

    Arminius goes to Basel, where they offer the PhD. title, but he refuses because he thought himself to be too young.

  7. 1584

    Geneva again

    Arminius returns to Geneva

  8. 1585

    Zurich

    Arminius travels to Zurich

  9. 1586

    Italy

    Arminius travles to Italy. His detractors accuse him of kissing the Pope's feet.

  10. 1588

    Ordination

    Arminius is ordained to the ministry in Amsterdam.

  11. 1590

    Wedding

    Arminius marries Elizabeth Real. In the same year some disagreements with the consistory begins.

  12. 1594

    Contribution to the city

    Arminius develops a new model for the schools of Amsterdam.

  13. 1599

    The anabaptists

    Arminius is tasked with writing a rebuttal to the Anabaptists.

  14. 1603

    Professor

    Arminius is appointed professor in Leiden and receive the PhD. in Divinity title.

  15. 1604

    The predestination

    Arminius presents his thesis about the predestination theme. Fraciscus Gomarus counters.

  16. 1605

    Dean

    Arminius becomes the Dean of the University of Leiden.

  17. 1608

    Acusations of romanism

    Arminius is acused of romanism. Him and Gomarus are summoned to present their views before the High Court. Arminius writes two Apologies and his Declaration of Sentiments

  18. 1609

    Death

    Arminius is seriously ill, and dies in october 19th.

That he was a Hollander, whom they that knew him, could not sufficiently esteem; Whom they that esteemed him not, did never sufficiently know.

Petrus Bertius
